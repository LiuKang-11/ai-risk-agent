# Contamination Triggers

## Trigger intent
Contamination signals must move quickly to critical handling because impact can propagate across lots and tools.

## Strong triggers
- near-full defect pattern
- dense random pattern across multiple wafers
- sudden spread to multiple chambers or tools sharing the same handling path
- matching abnormal particles, thickness drift, residue, or film anomalies
- multiple lots affected after shared maintenance, cassette, FOUP, or robot contact path

## Supporting triggers
- rising particle counts or metrology excursions
- cluster of alarms linked to vacuum, gas, purge, or chamber condition
- repeated edge-ring plus random contamination-like behavior after clean or PM
- operator notes indicating residue, contact marks, or environment event

## Required outcome
- classify as critical when one strong trigger exists with any corroboration
- classify as suspicious when only supporting triggers exist
- send immediate escalation to diagnosis when contamination is plausible
