wafer_defect_diagnosis_kb

Source package for the Wafer Diagnosis Agent.

========================================================================
README
========================================================================

Attach this knowledge source to the Wafer Diagnosis Agent.

Purpose:
- classify wafer defect patterns consistently
- connect defect geometry to likely process or equipment causes
- explain why a likely cause was chosen

This package should not decide approvals, blocked actions, or response ownership.

========================================================================
DEFECT TAXONOMY
========================================================================

Supported classes
- Center: defect concentration in the wafer center region
- Donut: annular ring around center with cleaner center and outer edge
- Edge-Loc: localized defect cluster near one edge sector
- Edge-Ring: continuous or near-continuous ring near wafer perimeter
- Loc: one or more compact local hotspots away from a simple ring
- Random: spatially dispersed defects without strong geometry
- Scratch: linear or curving streak consistent with contact damage
- Near-full: defects cover most of the wafer surface
- None: no meaningful defect pattern detected

Classification rules
- prefer the simplest class that explains the dominant geometry
- if two classes are both strong, return dominant class plus mixed-pattern note
- use None only when signal is absent, negligible, or clearly non-defect noise

Dominance heuristic
- dominant area coverage
- spatial concentration
- repeatability across wafers
- agreement between model output and extracted features

========================================================================
DEFECT GEOMETRY REFERENCE
========================================================================

Center
- highest density in central region
- usually radially symmetric or softly clustered

Donut
- ring-shaped concentration around center
- center remains cleaner than surrounding annulus

Edge-Loc
- concentrated patch near perimeter
- limited arc length rather than full-ring coverage

Edge-Ring
- continuous or broad annular band near edge
- often follows full circumference or most of it

Loc
- compact hotspot or several localized islands
- not constrained to center or edge geometry

Random
- low-structure, dispersed points
- weak center, edge, and linear scores

Scratch
- elongated line or curve
- often single-direction or contact-path aligned

Near-full
- dense coverage across most die positions
- often associated with contamination or catastrophic process excursion

None
- sparse or background-only pattern

Feature interpretation
- high edge density supports Edge-Loc or Edge-Ring
- high ring score supports Donut or Edge-Ring
- high scratch-line score supports Scratch
- high center density supports Center
- high randomness score with low structure supports Random

========================================================================
DEFECT TO CAUSE MAPPING
========================================================================

Center
- likely causes: uniform process non-idealities, center-biased deposition or etch, chuck or thermal effects
- check: recipe drift, temperature control, center-focused metrology excursion

Donut
- likely causes: radial non-uniformity, gas flow imbalance, edge-center process split, chamber condition
- check: radial thickness, etch rate, pressure-flow stability, chamber seasoning

Edge-Loc
- likely causes: local edge handling issue, partial seal problem, localized chamber contamination, notch-orientation sensitivity
- check: chamber orientation, edge handling path, wafer support contact points

Edge-Ring
- likely causes: edge bead, edge exclusion issue, edge process non-uniformity, chamber ring hardware, contamination at perimeter
- check: edge metrology, hardware wear, process edge controls

Loc
- likely causes: isolated particle event, local chuck issue, nozzle or showerhead non-uniformity, mask or focus issue
- check: hotspot repeatability by chamber and location

Random
- likely causes: contamination, unstable process, broad particle generation, upstream material issue
- check: particle trends, lot spread, maintenance history, environmental events

Scratch
- likely causes: robot arm contact, cassette or FOUP damage, contact surface wear, mechanical misalignment
- check: handling path, load port, end effector, repeated physical-contact history

Near-full
- likely causes: severe contamination, catastrophic tool excursion, gross recipe or chemistry failure
- check: immediate containment, shared tool path, multiple wafer impact

None
- likely causes: false positive, low defectivity, resolved transient
- check: confidence, feature strength, recent corroborating evidence

========================================================================
PROCESS STEP FAILURE MODES
========================================================================

Lithography
- local hotspots: focus, exposure, reticle contamination
- edge patterns: edge bead or handling-related issues
- random: particles, resist contamination, track instability

Etch
- center or donut: plasma non-uniformity, gas distribution, chamber seasoning drift
- edge-ring: chamber ring wear, edge exclusion interaction
- near-full: chemistry or chamber excursion

Deposition
- donut or edge-ring: flow distribution, wafer temperature, hardware condition
- random: particle shedding, precursor instability
- center: thermal or flow non-uniformity

CMP
- scratch: pad, carrier, slurry debris, handling damage
- edge patterns: retaining ring effects, pressure distribution
- random: slurry contamination

Implant
- center or ring-like signatures: beam or scan non-uniformity
- random: contamination or upstream wafer condition

Clean or inspection steps
- random or near-full: contamination spread
- scratch: transport or contact damage

Use rule
Process-step context should adjust cause ranking, but should not override strong geometry evidence without corroboration.

========================================================================
MIXED PATTERN GUIDANCE
========================================================================

When to mark a case as mixed
- top two classes are both plausible
- extracted features support more than one geometry strongly
- wafer map shows separate regions with different pattern types

Mixed-pattern workflow
- identify dominant class
- record secondary class
- explain which evidence supports each
- rank causes that could explain both patterns or a sequence of events

Common mixed cases
- Scratch plus Random: mechanical damage with contamination side effects
- Edge-Ring plus Random: edge process issue with contamination
- Center plus Donut: radial non-uniformity transitioning over time
- Loc plus Random: isolated hotspot inside a generally unstable process

Escalation hint
Mixed patterns should increase uncertainty and often justify metrology follow-up or human engineering review.

========================================================================
DIAGNOSTIC EXPLANATIONS
========================================================================

Explanation template
- predicted class
- confidence
- feature evidence
- contextual evidence
- likely causes
- why alternatives were deprioritized
- what evidence would strengthen or weaken the conclusion

Example 1
Predicted class: Scratch
Reasoning: strong scratch-line score, repeated alignment with handling path, recent robot alarm history, and limited evidence for contamination.
Likely causes: end-effector contact, cassette damage, or wafer transfer misalignment.

Example 2
Predicted class: Donut
Reasoning: ring score is high, center remains relatively clean, and similar issues recurred on the same chamber after maintenance.
Likely causes: radial process non-uniformity, chamber seasoning drift, or gas flow imbalance.

Example 3
Predicted class: Random
Reasoning: no dominant geometry, high randomness score, spread across wafers and lots, plus contamination indicators.
Likely causes: contamination source, broad particle generation, or unstable upstream process.

Explainability rules
- cite feature evidence before causal interpretation
- tie the cause ranking to process step and recent history
- explicitly note uncertainty when confidence is moderate or mixed
