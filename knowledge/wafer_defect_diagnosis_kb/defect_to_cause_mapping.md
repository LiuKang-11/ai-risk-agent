# Defect to Cause Mapping

## Center
- likely causes: uniform process non-idealities, center-biased deposition or etch, chuck or thermal effects
- check: recipe drift, temperature control, center-focused metrology excursion

## Donut
- likely causes: radial non-uniformity, gas flow imbalance, edge-center process split, chamber condition
- check: radial thickness, etch rate, pressure-flow stability, chamber seasoning

## Edge-Loc
- likely causes: local edge handling issue, partial seal problem, localized chamber contamination, notch-orientation sensitivity
- check: chamber orientation, edge handling path, wafer support contact points

## Edge-Ring
- likely causes: edge bead, edge exclusion issue, edge process non-uniformity, chamber ring hardware, contamination at perimeter
- check: edge metrology, hardware wear, process edge controls

## Loc
- likely causes: isolated particle event, local chuck issue, nozzle or showerhead non-uniformity, mask or focus issue
- check: hotspot repeatability by chamber and location

## Random
- likely causes: contamination, unstable process, broad particle generation, upstream material issue
- check: particle trends, lot spread, maintenance history, environmental events

## Scratch
- likely causes: robot arm contact, cassette or FOUP damage, contact surface wear, mechanical misalignment
- check: handling path, load port, end effector, repeated physical-contact history

## Near-full
- likely causes: severe contamination, catastrophic tool excursion, gross recipe or chemistry failure
- check: immediate containment, shared tool path, multiple wafer impact

## None
- likely causes: false positive, low defectivity, resolved transient
- check: confidence, feature strength, recent corroborating evidence
